How we met

Our journey began with a simple WhatsApp message: "Hey, this is Shalini here, regarding a marriage proposal." 💬 That message led to a phone call, and soon after, we had a brief video call where I waved a shy "hi" 👋 and there she was, laughing with her shiny white teeth, lighting up my screen 🌟. It was a vivid memory, I was at the airport ✈️, waiting for my flight, when we first saw each other on screen. From that very moment, we were hooked 💘.

Over the next two weeks, our daily chats and calls became the highlight of our days 🌞📱, each conversation bringing us closer together 🤗. Then came June 16th—the day we finally met in person! 🌸 While words felt unnecessary (we’d already shared so much through our countless conversations 😂), we both knew deep down that this was something truly special 💞.

Fast forward a few months to September 6th, when we celebrated our engagement 💍🎉! It was a day filled with joy, love, and dreams for the future 💑.

Now, we’re thrilled to invite you to our wedding on February 3rd, where we’ll begin this new chapter surrounded by the people we love ❤️.

Events

2nd February

Guests Reception 6PM - 10PM

It's one of the oldest traditional Indian pre wedding ceremony where the bride and groom attend the event together. This will be having a celebratory festival feel to it with people wearing vibrant clothes and dancing, singing, playing games and a lot more.

3rd February

Wedding 5AM-10AM

This is the main event in the entire wedding when the bride and the groom tie the knot. The groom comes with the barat and is greeted by the bride side with some dhol of course as maximum number of guests would be attending this event. This is when the couple circle the sacred fire and are finally declared husband and wife.

Mehndi 1PM - 5PM

Ethnic indian wear
Ladies can wear something bright & vivid and gentlemen can be on their vibrant kurtas with pants or pyjamas along with an optional shawl.

Cocktail Night 7PM - 12AM

Western attire
A semi-formal look that is contemporary & fashionable which is not too prim & proper is the best to pull off this night. You can see some pins for ladies & gents.

Wedding 6PM-12AM

Indo-western
Ladies can flaunt their unconventional sarees, lehengas, anarkalis, dresses and the list goes on whereas gentlemen can wear any kind of suit or just blazer. If you don't like to wear a suit/blazer, you can do a high-neck (band gala) waistcoat & shirt combo.
